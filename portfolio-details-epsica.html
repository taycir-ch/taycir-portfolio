Epsica - Mobile health AI ecosystem

Project Overview & Clinical Context

Epsica is an award-winning biomedical software ecosystem engineered for early neuro-developmental risk monitoring and real-time medical alerting. Designed as a wearable-connected mobile solution, it aims to protect vulnerable patients by closing the gap between physiological raw data and emergency clinical responsiveness.

IoT Architecture & Predictive Analytics

The platform architecture establishes a real-time data pipeline streaming biological signals from integrated smartwatch sensors to a secure mobile core via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). Built using modern mobile software development patterns, the system leverages background processing queues and lightweight predictive analytics models to execute anomaly detection. It constantly evaluates physiological thresholds to trigger instant push notifications and critical alerts for caregivers and emergency teams.

Human-Centered Impact & High Recognition

As the Project Lead, I merged my computational skills with cognitive science and clinical observation patterns to design an intuitive, high-stress-tolerant interface for medical monitoring. The project successfully solved complex UX challenges around real-time medical dashboarding and data transparency for doctors. Reflecting its profound technical viability and public health potential, Epsica was awarded the 2nd National Innovation Prize for HealthTech.
